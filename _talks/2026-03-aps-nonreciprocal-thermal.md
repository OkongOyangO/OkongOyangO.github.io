Contributed oral talk, session *Optical, Acoustic/Elastic, Thermal, and Nanophononic Metamaterials II*.

**Abstract:** Nonreciprocal thermal photonics enables directional control of heat transfer and radiative energy conversion through broken reciprocity in material or structural response. In this work, we reveal topology-enhanced nonreciprocal thermal emission in magnetic topological materials using ab initio calculations, demonstrating that intrinsic topological effects can induce strong nonreciprocity even without an external magnetic field. By developing a first-principles toolbox, we computed nonreciprocal thermal radiation through ab initio calculation of the optical dielectric tensor and electromagnetic modeling. We further predict a series of real materials exhibiting pronounced nonreciprocal effects in the mid-infrared range due to their topological band structures. Our work establishes a quantitative framework connecting ab initio electronic structure to nonreciprocal photonic phenomena, opening avenues for discovering new materials and developing novel directional thermal devices.

Authors: Yiyang Jiang (Penn State University), Binghai Yan (Penn State University), Linxiao Zhu (Penn State University).

Related publication: Jiang, Y., Zhao, Y., Zhu, L., & Yan, B. (2026). *Designing Strong and Broadband Nonreciprocal Thermal Radiation in Magnetic Topological Materials.* [arXiv:2606.14861](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14861).
